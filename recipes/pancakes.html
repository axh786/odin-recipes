Pancakes

Description

Pancakes are an extremely popular flat cake that can trace its origins to the ancient greeks. Today in North America, pancakes are treated as a breakfast food known for its thickness and fluffiness. North American pancakes start off as a batter made of flour, sugar, butter, milk and eggs which is then cooked on a hot frying pan or griddle greased by butter or oil. After they are made, the flat cakes are then assembled in a stack, drizzled with honey or syrup, and a slice of butter on top.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt, or more to taste
  • 1 ¼ cups milk, set at room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg, set at room temperature
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla

Steps

  1. Sift flour twice, baking powder, sugar, and salt together in a large bowl.
  2. In another bowl, beat your egg add melted butter and beat lightly until belnded then lightly beat in your milk & vanilla (careful, do not over beat your egg)
  3. Add liquid mixture to the dry mixture and lightly mix.
  4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or pan over medium-high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake; cook until bubbles form and the edges are dry, about 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and cook until browned on the other side. Repeat with remaining batter.
