Pancakes

Description

Pancakes are an extremely popular flat cake that can trace its origins to the ancient greeks. Today in North America, pancakes are treated as a breakfast food known for its thickness and fluffiness. North American pancakes start off as a batter made of flour, sugar, butter, milk and eggs which is then cooked on a hot frying pan or griddle greased by butter or oil. After they are made, the flat cakes are then assembled in a stack, drizzled with honey or syrup, and a slice of butter on top.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt, or more to taste
  • 1 ¼ cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
